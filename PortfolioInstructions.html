Instruction Design & Copy
Evolution in Interactive Ebooks

Business Partner

National Science Teaching Association (NSTA)

Product

Instruction use in K-5 interactive science ebooks via pop-ups and embedded text.

My Role

UX design and copy.

Outcome

The results were instructions embedded in the narrative text as well as dedicated pop-up instructions. The instructions now focus on the act of interacting with the simulation without giving any hints to the answers.

The Opportunity

90% of my time at conferences, I spent watching teachers interact with the book. During that time, I noticed how instructions could be clarified, simultaneously easing the narrative flow. Although adult conference attendees were not actively struggling to understand the instructions, they did take more time than I thought necessary as an adult, indicating a child–the target demographic– would have even more trouble. I saw this as an opportunity to clarify the instructions and make them more seamless in the story's narrative.

Tablets at NSTA conference where teachers would interact with the ebooks

Pilot Ebook

Initially, the narrative text included the instructions, and the interactivity of an element was not inherently obvious. Although a hand icon–taken from our previous line of ebooks– was used to indicate interactivity, the implementation was inconsistent.

Pilot ebook with legacy hand-icon

First In Series

After a focus group of our pilot ebook, the instruction designer and I troubleshot how to improve the instructions based on feedback. We wanted to ensure the user could check what they were supposed to do on each page.

Initially, we discussed if instructions should only be within the narrative text. However, too many instructions would impede the flow of the story and may not be necessary for repetitive interactive elements.

After the focus group, I saw consistently using the hand-icon in the ebook would create a host of issues. Producing unhelpful visual clutter, visually date the look quickly, and rely on instructions in the text– which we had agreed previously would impede the narrative.

The instruction designer and I solutionized on a pop up with instructions appearing on every interactive page. Next, we figured out where to place the pop-up. For the least impact on the content layout, the pop-up button went next to the page number as a separate element. The page number itself could have been the pop-up element, but since K-5 students were the target audience, UI intuitiveness out-weighed style. I designed a hand icon, signifying touch, and following the visual queues of general interactive buttons.

Location of pop-up button next to page number

Sixth In Series

After the release of the sixth ebook, I implemented two changes.

I widened the width of the pop-ups' boxes, allowing single sentence instructions to be easier to read.

Before: pop-up boxes were often narrow covering main screen text and decreasing legibility

After: pop-up boxes were widened not blocking main screen text and allowing easier legibilty–it is important the user read all the instructions carefully

I removed any instructions insinuating right and wrong answers or extraneous information already covered in the narrative text in the instruction pop-up.

Before: pop-up box instructions had extraneous text increasing reading time. In this image the narrative text says to match the objects in the shadow, therefore it is not entirely necssary in the pop-up box instructions. Simply saying "Drag the object onto the tables" would be enough.

After: pop-up box instructions did not have repetitive information covered in the narrative text

These changes sped up and increased comprehension enough of conference attendees to mark a difference.

However, the frequency and style of embedded instructions in the narrative were still inconsistent between ebooks.

There was still room for improvement. The next obstacle to tackle was making instructions so easy to find and understand that if someone opened a book to any page, the user would be able to know what was interactive and what they needed to do.

Sixth To Thirteenth in Series

From the sixth to the thirteenth release, I fine-tuned standardizing embedded instructions–copy I wrote and integrated. General instructions became part of the story not added on at the end of the narrative. The pop-up instructions told the user exactly what to do without giving any hints to correct answers.

After: pop-up box instructions pertained to interactivity only and instructions were embedded seemlessly into the narrative text

Results

Due to budget constraints, we could not retroactively update previous books' instructions. However, at conferences, I was able to monitor which books invoked the most questions and which ones garnered the least–later releases created the least questions.
